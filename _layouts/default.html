Real-Time Scribble Shading

Replacing the Standard Lighting Model with a Stylized Hatching Effect

Summary

This project introduces a system that evokes the visual style of pencil-drawn imagery by replacing the standard lighting model with a hatching effect that reacts to lighting in real-time. Texturing is done in object-space, meaning that hatching strokes will bend around 3D models to further communicate form and depth.

This effect is primarily achieved by generating and implementing hierarchical hatching textures called Tonal Art Maps (TAMS), first introduced by Praun et al., which adhere to a "nesting stroke property" that preserves stroke locations across tone and mipmap levels.

By combining different TAMs together during texturing, an interesting scribble effect is achieved. Additional effects are implemented through the use of noise, Sobel filter outlines, and more.

All scripts and shaders were created by Adam Khaddaj.
